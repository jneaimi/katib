Architecture in 30 seconds

A single entry-point service receives requests, writes to a primary database, and dispatches notifications via a queued worker. Failure of the worker never blocks the primary write path — notifications are eventually-consistent by design.

Key decision

We deliberately chose eventual consistency for notifications so the write path stays fast even during downstream outages. Don't reverse this without revisiting the original ADR.

Rollback

deploy rollback --service <name> --to-version <previous-sha>

Rollback completes in under two minutes. Customer-facing errors usually clear within a minute of the rollback finishing.

Health checks

  • Primary service: /healthz on the public endpoint
  • Worker: depth of the notification queue (stays under 100 in steady state)
  • Database: latency panel on the shared dashboard

Known issues

Medium
Notification retries can duplicate in rare race conditions
Affects about 1 in 10,000 notifications. De-duplication is planned for next quarter. Acceptable in the meantime because consumers are idempotent.
Low
Admin dashboard is slow for tenants with more than 50k records
Pagination works around it. A proper index is queued for the next perf sprint.
Low
Localized date formats are inconsistent in the export CSV
Cosmetic. Tracked in issue #1247; fix is a one-liner whenever someone picks it up.
