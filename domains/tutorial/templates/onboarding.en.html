Part I

Welcome aboard

A brief tour of the company, how we work, and why your role matters to the mission we're building toward.

Who we are

We build tools that help people think more clearly. Our team is small, distributed, and deliberate — we ship less but we ship what matters.

How we work

  • Async-first: most coordination happens in writing; meetings are reserved for decisions that need a real-time conversation.
  • Documented decisions: every non-trivial choice gets an ADR, so the reasoning survives turnover.
  • Ship-learn-adjust: we prefer small bets we can observe over grand plans we can't verify.
Why this matters

The async-first rhythm means your first reflex should be a written update, not a meeting request. That single habit will unblock you and your teammates faster than anything else.

Our mission

Build the calmest, clearest thinking tools we can — ones that reward patient use and age gracefully alongside the people using them.

Responsibilities

  • Flag risks early; late surprises cost much more than early concerns.

Part III

Tools and environment

Everything you need installed, authenticated, and verified before you touch real code.

Accounts to set up

  • Company email and calendar
  • Source control (with 2FA enabled on day one)
  • Chat workspace and your team's channels
  • Password manager and any shared vaults you need
  • Time-tracking and expense tools, if applicable

Software to install

  1. Install the core toolchain

    Follow the repo's INSTALL.md. When in doubt, prefer the exact versions listed rather than the latest.

  2. Clone the main repo and run it locally

    Success means you see the app running on your machine at the expected port, with no red errors in the console.

  3. Run the test suite once

    A green run proves your environment matches CI. If tests fail locally but pass in CI, that's a sign to pair with a teammate before debugging solo.

Security basics

Turn on 2FA everywhere on day one. Never paste credentials into chat, even in DMs. If you need to share a secret, use the team's secrets manager.

Part IV

Your first week

A day-by-day walkthrough so nothing important slips, and so you can relax into a steady rhythm.

Day 1 — Meet and explore

  • 1:1 with your manager and onboarding buddy
  • Intro to the team in the shared channel
  • Read the architecture overview end-to-end (no code yet)

Day 2 — Environment

  • Install the toolchain; run the app locally
  • Green test run on your machine
  • First small commit to a scratch branch

Day 3 — First change

  • Pick a starter issue from the "good-first-issue" list
  • Open a draft PR early; invite review as you go
  • Document anything surprising you ran into

Day 4 — Ship

  • Address review feedback and merge your first change
  • Watch it roll out in the live environment
  • Write a short retro of what slowed you down

Day 5 — Retro and plan

  • 1:1 with manager to reflect on the week
  • Agree on the focus for weeks 2 and 3
  • Share one "I wish I'd known" note to improve this onboarding
